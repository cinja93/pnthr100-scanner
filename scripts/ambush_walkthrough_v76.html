1. What Ambush is, in one paragraph

Ambush is an automated trading engine. It watches a fixed list of about 300 stocks (the "AI 300"), and it only buys or sells when several conditions line up at once: a longer-term trend is in place, the trade's sector is moving the right way, a short-term breakout is happening today, and the price has just popped through a precise hourly trigger. When they agree, it takes a position in the direction of the trend — buying breakouts up (a "long") or short-selling breakdowns (a "short"). It starts small and adds only as the trade proves itself, and it always carries a protective stop that trails behind the price and tightens as the trade works, so a winner can't turn back into a loser. It checks everything against your real Interactive Brokers ("IBKR") account every minute and treats that account as the absolute truth. It runs only while the U.S. market is open.

3. The heartbeat — how often it thinks
  • Once every 60 seconds, Mon–Fri, only while the market is open (9:30 AM–about 4:05 PM ET). Otherwise it does nothing.
  • Only one copy ever acts each minute — a database lock guarantees it, so an order can never be placed twice.
  • It can be switched off with a single setting. It saves all state between ticks, so it survives restarts without losing any position.
4. The green lights before a NEW entry

A stock has to pass all of these at the same moment, all pointing the same direction, before Ambush opens a new position:

  1. Weekly trend (the slow filter). Using a proprietary sector-tuned moving average and the PNTHR signal system, the stock has an active Buy-Long (or Sell-Short) weekly signal. This decides which of the ~300 names may trade today, and in which direction.
  2. Sector agrees (directional). Each AI sector is rated BULL or BEAR by its own 5-day return — positive = BULL, negative = BEAR (the badge on the Sectors AI page). A long is only allowed in a BULL sector; a short only in a BEAR sector.
  3. Daily breakout (today's confirmation). Live price is above the higher of the prior two days' highs + a penny (long) / below the lower of the prior two lows − a penny (short).
  4. Hourly trigger (the exact moment). Live price breaks above the most-recent completed hourly bar's high + a penny (long) / below its low − a penny (short). The earliest a new entry can fire is after the first hour completes (10:30).

A broad market-direction (regime) reading is calculated but turned off in this version — Ambush takes both longs and shorts.

The five-lot pyramid (with the 10% cap on every add)

The full size is split into five lots, added only as price moves in favor:

Only Lot 1 goes on at entry; Lots 2–5 add automatically as the trade climbs. Every add re-checks the 10%-of-NAV cap: if the next lot would push the position past 10% of NAV, the engine trims the top lots (Lot 5, then 4) so the position stays at or under 10% — which also keeps the average cost lower. Costs (slippage, commission, short borrow) are always included.

6. The life of a trade — six stages
StageWhat it means
① STALKINGEvery name with a live weekly signal, waiting for the daily breakout.
② HUNTINGDaily breakout cleared; watching for the hourly trigger.
③ ATTACKTrigger fired; the order goes out next tick.
④ DEVOURA confirmed position is held, protective stop live at the broker.
⑤ STILL HUNGRYA name that exited and is hunting a re-entry. Shows the frozen price it must hold above.
⑥ PROTECTThe trailing stop has climbed above average cost — the trade can no longer lose money.
7. The exit — the two-bar hourly trailing stop
Long: stop = the lowest low of the last two completed hourly bars, minus a penny.
Short: stop = the highest high of the last two completed hourly bars, plus a penny.
  • It trails up only: as the trend climbs the two-bar low rises and the stop follows. It never slides back down.
  • The level is set from the last two completed bars, but the exit is live — the instant price drops through it, even mid-hour, the resting stop fires.
  • Once the stop trails above average cost, the position flips to PROTECT and can't lose money.
  • It governs from entry (no $75 break-even snap). A brand-new entry's first stop is the first-hour low (the disaster floor) until the two-bar trail rises above it.
8. Getting back in — a re-entry (the Rev 6 fix)

When a position exits, the name drops to STILL HUNGRY and can be re-entered — but only on a strict set of checks whose whole purpose is: only get back in if the trade is genuinely still working, and never churn.

  1. The weekly signal is still on. If the weekly Buy-Long / Sell-Short has ended, the name is dropped.
  2. The sector still agrees (BULL for a long, BEAR for a short).
  3. Price is still above the FROZEN original daily breakout. The 2-day-high level that first qualified the trade this cycle is frozen (e.g. $463.89) and held — not re-figured each day. Re-enter only while price holds above it; if it falls below, wait; if it reclaims, eligible again. This stops us re-buying a trade that's rolling over. (Resets only when a new weekly cycle begins.)
  4. The re-entry bar CLOSES GREEN above the prior bar's high (long) / closes red below the prior bar's low (short). This is the decisive Rev 6 change. A brand-new entry uses the lighter intraday poke; a re-entry, because you're jumping back into a trade mid-move, requires the stronger confirmed close. Without it the engine re-enters on every intraday wiggle and churns itself to death on commissions; with it, the same strategy is a strong, defensible winner.

The tighter stop on a re-entry

  • The stop starts at the low of the entry bar (the bar it broke in on) — tight. Dip below it and you're out for a very small loss, free to re-enter on the next confirmed break (no waiting period).
  • Held through the entry bar and the next two completed hourly bars, then the normal two-bar trailing stop takes over.

11. Capital and withdrawals

Start $100K. Each time the working balance reaches $2,000,000, withdraw $1,000,000 (banked, locked, out of trading), and keep trading with the remaining ~$1M; bank another $1M every time it climbs back to $2M. Working capital stays between $1M and $2M — realistic capacity. Total return counts the banked capital (total value = banked + working). No leverage: deployed never exceeds available cash.
